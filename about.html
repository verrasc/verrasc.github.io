Why Am I Here?

As a child, I made several attempts to write my own dictionary. I was compelled to compile information into bite-sized pieces for maximum absorption. The dictionary was the first dataset I had really encountered at 7 years old. I always found it weird that I did that, but looking back it makes sense now. The bad news: It was never published. Nor was it completed for that matter. I never made it past the letter ‘C’. The good news: It wouldn't have sold many copies. After travelling as a vagabond musician for an era, my love of geography, place, and culture was developed. Travelling the world, finding the commonality of it all. Or what made a place special. The experience was eventually distilled into one question: Why here? It became a social and philosophical journey, a rewarding aspect to a poor musicians life. Eventually, I put this love into action, and was grateful to learn a person can love their job.
