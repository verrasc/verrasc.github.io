As a child, I made several attempts to write my own dictionary. I was curiously compelled to format information. I tell my friends this story in a way that hasn't always been kind to the 8-year-old me. But looking back now, it makes perfect sense. The dictionary was the first tangible dataset I had really encountered and I enjoyed interacting with it. Surprisingly, I never finished the dictionary. But had it been published, it would have been a bestseller.

Cut to: Whilst travelling the continent as a vagabond musician for an era, my love of geography, place, and culture was developed. Each stop was a review of what place means. The experience was eventually distilled into one question: Why here? It became a social and philosophical journey, a rewarding aspect to a poor musician's life. I put this love into action and found a job that requires creativity and datasets! How lucky am I?
(BTW - I finish all of my project now)
